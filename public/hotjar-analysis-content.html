Executive Summary

QBTraining.com generated $1.7 million in revenue from 2,544 purchases across 319,718 sessions between November 2025 and February 2026. The average order value is $682, confirming the Certification Plan ($699.95) as the likely best-seller.

Analysis of 250,000+ Hotjar interactions and GA4 data reveals a site with enormous untapped potential. Desktop generates 88% of revenue ($1.3M) but suffers from 20–39% dead click rates. Meanwhile, mobile serves as the research channel with excellent engagement but low direct conversion — expected behavior for a $700 product.

At $600–$1,000 per plan: Nobody impulse-buys on their phone. Visitors make multiple visits before purchasing. Trust is non-negotiable. The comparison table is the most important element on the site. FAQ engagement is massive because people have lots of questions before committing $700.

Five Systemic Problems the Data Revealed

1. The Homepage Is Hemorrhaging Visitors Critical

Only 9.9% of desktop users reach the halfway point of the homepage. The page is 9,346px tall. There is a catastrophic 40.2% drop-off between 5–10% of the page. At $682 AOV, each percentage point of improvement represents hundreds of potential purchases per quarter.

2. Desktop Dead Clicks Are Epidemic Critical

20–39% of ALL desktop clicks hit non-interactive elements. People click on body text, comparison table cells, section backgrounds, and card areas expecting interactivity. This is the purchase channel — the device generating 88% of revenue — and up to 4 in 10 clicks are wasted.

4. Sign In Dominates the Homepage High

30.6% of all desktop homepage clicks are Sign In. The homepage functions more as a login portal than a landing page. After removing Sign In and dead clicks, only ~46% of desktop clicks are productive prospect engagement.

5. Self-Paced Courses: Conversion Black Hole High

26.5% of clicks go to course cards, but only 4.6% ever reach a CTA. People browse thinking they’re buying individual courses, not understanding the plan model. The "Load More" button gets 1,154 clicks from people treating it like a course catalog.

Site-Wide Recommendations

SW-1: Make the Comparison Table Interactive Priority #1
Effort: Medium • Impact: Highest

1,513 clicks (7.3% of Plans & Pricing clicks) hit comparison table text. At $682 AOV, this is the single most important decision-making element on the site.

  • Make each feature row expandable with 2–3 sentence descriptions
  • Add hover states on desktop (row highlights, cursor: pointer)
  • On mobile: tap to expand/collapse with smooth animation
  • Add “What’s this?” icon next to each feature name
SW-2: Fix Desktop Hover States & Click Affordances Priority #2
Effort: Low–Medium • Impact: High

20–39% of ALL desktop clicks hit non-interactive elements.

Element TypeCSS Treatment
Interactive elementscursor: pointer + hover transition
Non-interactive textcursor: default, no hover changes
Cardshover:box-shadow or hover:transform(translateY)
Table rowshover:background-color highlight
Buttonshover:background-color darken + slight scale
SW-3: Add Aggregate Star Rating + Review Count Priority #3
Effort: Low • Impact: Medium–High

Add to the trust bar: “4.7 stars from 1,581+ reviews.” Include Schema.org AggregateRating markup for rich snippets. Display on every page.

SW-4: Add Cross-Device Journey Bridges Priority #4
Effort: Medium • Impact: Medium–High

MVP: “Email me this comparison” button on Plans & Pricing. Captures leads AND bridges devices.

Full version: “Continue on your computer” prompt, URL reinforcement near mobile CTAs, SMS self-link on decision pages.

The 40.2% desktop drop-off at 5–10% scroll must be investigated. On a 1920x1080 monitor, the first viewport should show: headline, value prop, CTA, and trust bar.

“Start Learning”: green, filled, prominent. “Sign In”: text-only or outline, smaller. They must NOT look like equal-weight options.

Design Principles (From the Data)

#Principle
1Desktop is the purchase channel — protect it. 88% of revenue.
2Mobile is the research channel — optimize for it. Make comparison easy, add “email me this.”
3Short pages convert. 4,000px max. The data is unambiguous.
4Every browseable item needs a CTA. At $682 AOV, every missed click = potential $700 loss.
5Make it obvious what’s clickable. Fix the 20–39% dead click epidemic.

9Sign In is a utility, not navigation.
